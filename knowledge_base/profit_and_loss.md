
# Profit & Loss Statement (P&L)

## What is a Profit & Loss Statement?
A Profit & Loss (P&L) statement, also called an Income Statement, is a financial report that summarizes revenues, costs, and expenses over a specific period (monthly, quarterly, or annually). It shows whether a business is profitable.

## Structure of a P&L Statement

### 1. Revenue (Income)
Total money earned from business activities.
- Sales Revenue
- Service Income
- Other Income

### 2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs of producing goods or services sold.
- Raw materials
- Direct labor
- Manufacturing overhead

### 3. Gross Profit
**Gross Profit = Revenue – COGS**
Indicates profitability before operating expenses.

### 4. Operating Expenses
Indirect costs of running the business:
- Rent and utilities
- Salaries and wages
- Marketing and advertising
- Depreciation
- Insurance

### 5. Operating Profit (EBIT)
**Operating Profit = Gross Profit – Operating Expenses**

### 6. Net Profit (Bottom Line)
**Net Profit = Operating Profit – Taxes – Interest**
The final profit after all deductions.

## Key Metrics
- **Gross Margin %** = (Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100
- **Net Margin %** = (Net Profit / Revenue) × 100
- **Break-even Point** = Fixed Costs / (Price – Variable Cost per Unit)

## Why It Matters
- Measures business performance over time
- Helps identify cost-cutting opportunities
- Required by investors, banks, and tax authorities
- Used for budgeting and forecasting
