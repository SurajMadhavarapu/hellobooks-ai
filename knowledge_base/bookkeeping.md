# Bookkeeping

## What is Bookkeeping?
Bookkeeping is the systematic process of recording, organizing, and maintaining financial transactions of a business. It forms the foundation of accounting and helps businesses track income and expenses accurately.

## Key Concepts

### Double-Entry System
Every transaction affects at least two accounts. For every debit, there is an equal and opposite credit.
- Example: When you buy supplies for $100 cash → Debit Supplies $100, Credit Cash $100

### Chart of Accounts
A structured list of all accounts used by a business:
- Assets (Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory)
- Liabilities (Accounts Payable, Loans)
- Equity (Owner's Capital, Retained Earnings)
- Revenue (Sales, Service Income)
- Expenses (Rent, Salaries, Utilities)

### General Ledger
The master record of all financial transactions, organized by account.

### Trial Balance
A summary report that lists all accounts and their balances to verify debits equal credits.

## Common Bookkeeping Tasks
- Recording daily transactions
- Reconciling bank statements
- Managing accounts payable and receivable
- Processing payroll
- Generating financial reports

## Best Practices
- Record transactions promptly and accurately
- Keep business and personal finances separate
- Reconcile accounts monthly
- Maintain organized receipts and documentation
- Use accounting software for efficiency
