
# Invoices

## What is an Invoice?
An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, indicating the products or services provided, quantities, and agreed prices. It serves as a formal request for payment.

## Key Components of an Invoice
1. **Invoice Number** – Unique identifier for tracking
2. **Invoice Date** – Date the invoice was issued
3. **Due Date** – When payment is expected
4. **Seller Information** – Name, address, contact details
5. **Buyer Information** – Client name, address
6. **Line Items** – Description, quantity, unit price
7. **Subtotal** – Sum before tax
8. **Tax** – GST, VAT, or applicable taxes
9. **Total Amount Due** – Final amount payable
10. **Payment Terms** – Net 30, Net 60, etc.

## Types of Invoices
- **Proforma Invoice** – Preliminary bill sent before work begins
- **Standard Invoice** – Most common; sent after delivery
- **Recurring Invoice** – Sent on a scheduled basis (monthly subscriptions)
- **Credit Invoice (Credit Note)** – Issued for refunds or corrections
- **Debit Invoice** – Increases amount owed by the buyer

## Payment Terms
- **Net 30** – Payment due within 30 days
- **Net 60** – Payment due within 60 days
- **Due on Receipt** – Payment expected immediately
- **2/10 Net 30** – 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise due in 30

## Invoice Best Practices
- Always include a unique invoice number
- Send invoices promptly after service delivery
- Follow up on overdue invoices politely but firmly
- Keep copies of all issued invoices
- Use professional invoice templates
